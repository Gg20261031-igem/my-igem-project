Abstract
Chronic diarrhea affects millions worldwide, yet its underlying causes often remain undiagnosed due to the lack of accessible diagnostic tools. One of the most overlooked conditions is Bile Acid Malabsorption (BAM), a disorder in which excess bile acids reach the colon, leading to persistent diarrhea, abdominal pain, and impaired quality of life.

While occasional diarrhea is an experience everyone shares, approximately 5% of adults suffer from chronic diarrhea. Due to its perceived "harmless" nature, chronic diarrhea receives limited attention in clinical diagnostics and therapeutic development.
